How We Calculate Detection Confidence

At BrowSafe, our detection confidence score (0%–100%) tells you how certain we are that a website is safe or unsafe. This score is designed to be reliable and transparent, combining advanced technology with smart analysis to protect you from phishing, malware, and other online threats.

  • Google Safe Browsing (70% of the Score): We use Google’s Safe Browsing API, trusted by billions of devices worldwide, to check if a website is listed as dangerous. It scans for known phishing, malware, and social engineering threats, giving us a highly accurate starting point.
  • URL Feature Analysis (30% of the Score): Our custom system examines the website’s URL for suspicious traits, such as unusual length, special characters, or keywords like “login” or “secure.” This helps catch new threats that might not yet be in Google’s database.
  • Combined Confidence: We blend these two scores, with a heavier weight on Google’s proven technology, to create a final confidence score. To ensure reliability, we cap the score between 10% and 90%, reflecting real-world uncertainties.

Example: If a website is flagged for phishing by Google Safe Browsing and has a suspicious URL, we might show a “Detection Confidence: 70%” that it’s unsafe, meaning there’s a strong chance it’s dangerous. For safe websites, you’ll see a higher score, like 90%. This score helps you make informed decisions while browsing.
